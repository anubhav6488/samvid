Frequently asked  questions

Will you support or reimburse our travel expenses?

Since CodeLoom  2024 is a community-driven hackathon, unfortunately we will not be able to support you with travel.

Is it mandatory to stay at the venue during the hackathon? how long am I allowed to leave the premises?

Yes, once you are checked-in for the hackathon, it is mandatory to stay at the venue. You can only leave after the hackathon ends, i.e. on Mar 12 after the closing ceremony.

My parents/guardian will be coming along with me. can they stay at the venue?

The answer is No. Arrangements like sleeping area, food, etc., are only for the attendees. We recommend you to find a stay option for them near the venue. They can drop you at the hackathon venue on 9th Feb and can pick you on 11th Feb but can't stay at venue during the hackathon as we do not have any provision for them.

What are the arrangements for hackers?

On the nights 11 March we will have a supervised sleeping space for hackers to rest and sleep at the venue. We have arranged a separate sleeping area for girls, clean washrooms, personal hygiene and sanitary kits, etc. We recommend bringing your own blanket if you need one. We want to make sure that all hackers feel comfortable and safe at the hackathon.

At what time should I arrive at the hackathon on 11 March and what time can I depart on 12 March?

ou are expected to arrive at the hackathon venue by 10 AM IST on Sunday, 11 Mar. The estimated departure time from the venue is 1 PM IST on Monday, 12th Mar. Book your return travel tickets accordingly to ensure you don't miss the Closing Ceremony.

Where can I find the judging criteria? how many judges found should I be prepared for?

You can find everything you need to know about judging including the criteria and the structure in the judging guidelines in the Hacker Guide sent to you via email.
